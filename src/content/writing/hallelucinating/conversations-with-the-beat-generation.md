Patti Smith is like one of those friends that bombard you with references to obscure poets and authors when you talk with them, not to appear knowledgeable but because they are genuinely passionate about art. Reading this book felt like having a conversation, one in which she casually says things like 'So there I was, hanging out with my dear friend William S. Burroughs'.

I didn't know Patti as a torchbearer for the Beat generation, but the book is also fascinating from that viewpoint (she's the only female Beat poet I know of). A lot of 'M Train' is stream-of-consciousness if one is being kind and scatterbrained if one is not, so it's perhaps not for everyone. But if you follow her down the rabbit hole, where she jumps from talking about Murakami, to a dream she had last night, to the repetitive and meditative rituals of a writer, to her interest in (what some might consider to be mundane) American detective shows, you will be entertained and at the very least, have enough book/poetry recommendations to last you a couple of years.
