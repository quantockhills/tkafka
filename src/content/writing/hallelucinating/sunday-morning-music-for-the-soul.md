'This Album Changed My Life'

For AIR's Moon Safari, and Talkie Walkie

Written by Anupama Nair.

AIR literally came to me like a breath of fresh air (sorry) when I was 12 years old and stumbled upon this CD that had 5 albums by AIR burned on to it, that my sister's 12th grade best friend gave to her to listen to. I saw it lying around, got curious and played it out loud on the old Sony music player that was my lifeline since I had lost my beloved walkman. And it truly changed my life.

Moon Safari and Talkie Walkie together made me realise how incredible it is to listen to entire albums, truly a revelation for a preteen who would mostly listen to hit pop songs and some of her father's old music on shuffle. It felt like I rediscovered music.

The opening double of Moon Safari is enough to have anyone intrigued. Each song sets either a vibrant, breezy montage of videos in my mind that look just like the music sounds. From La Femme d'Argent to Sexy Boy (hilarious video; do check it out!) to All I Need (one of my favourite love songs ever) till the very last track, the music transports you to the minds of its creators. And I can never get enough of it.

The duo, Jean-Benoît Dunckel and Nicolas Godin, describe the album as "this alien, psychedelic, loungecore music you’d listen to on a Sunday morning after you’d been out clubbing the night before." Well, I'd listen to it anytime. And I strongly recommend it to you too. Off to a good start with this one, I assure you.
