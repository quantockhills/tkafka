Saw this in a second hand bookstore in Berlin and I identified the author's name as it was mentioned in a song I loved - 'the past is a grotesque animal' by of Montreal, an 11 minute long psychedelic dance romp about the narrators urge to fuck things up, and a relentless examination of their relationships in the past.

Bataille takes this investigation up a notch - the prose is gorgeous, but so, so depraved. It's a fever dream of a novella, one that is over before you have the time to process it properly.

Oh and the song is worth a listen as well, here's a lyric that connects it nicely to the book:

'It's like we weren't made for this world, though I wouldn't really want to meet someone who was.'
