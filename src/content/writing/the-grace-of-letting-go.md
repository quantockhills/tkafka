Carrie and Lowell by Sufjan Stevens

Carrie and Lowell was written following the demise of Carrie, Sufjans mother.

So Sufjan said in an interview how the songs were essentially therapy for him; while writing songs, he'd write down everything he was feeling at the time honestly.

And so he goes from anger to regret to confusion (directed towards his mother, who had left him when he was young), to forgiveness, grief, and acceptance. He'll talk about the things that make life worth living and the beauty in nature and people he didn't appreciate, but not before contemplating whether he has a reason to live.

It is, strangely enough, life affirming. But not before it takes you through a rollercoaster of emotions.
