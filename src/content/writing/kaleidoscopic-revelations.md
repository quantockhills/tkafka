This album review is for In Rainbows by Radiohead. Written (and illustrated) by Jai, it's something I could relate to in a massive way, being a big fan of the band myself.

'Radiohead and I have a very old history. The first Radiohead song that I heard was house of cards and I was all of 12. I couldn't relate to it at all. The distorted, wavy figures and RBG patterns in the music video weirded me out. I loved pop music back then. Then at 14 I heard my second Radiohead song, 'Lotus Flower'. Again, I was intrigued but not enough. I remember trying to listen to them after this really attractive senior from my school said that they were very good. I don't know where she is now. God I had a huge crush on her. Then I turned 17 and it was the time of my experimentation with a lot of drugs. When I dropped acid for the first time, I didn't know what music should I listen to. Next day I went and asked this guy about it. He suggested Radiohead. I dropped acid again at 2 PM in the Delhi heat, traveling in an auto rickshaw. It felt like the movie Tron but with a lot of bright yellow light. Everything became florescent. The patterns were overwhelming and I decided to watch the house of cards video again. Boy did it make sense. After that I heard the entire album, while sitting in a park in Munirka Enclave. My math class was at 5 and I just wanted to peak before I went.

Right from 15 Step to weird fishes to All I need, each song's a journey. In the sky I could see all kinds of manifestations of clouds. When 'Videotape' came, I saw an old CRT TV with a VCR player, the screen showing my childhood in a very kaleidoscopic way. It wasn't there, yet I could see it. Again, acid trips are very personal. This album which had a huge impact on me; I was getting over my first break up and I was in class 12th and in that state I was experimenting with LSD. In Rainbows healed me and helped me develop a taste in experimental music. It was the zenith for me; I couldn't appreciate any other rock act for a while. Can you find a better act than them?'
