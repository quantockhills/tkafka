So Anupama wrote a really fun piece about one of her (and my) favorites, The Cure's 'Disintegration'! Disintegration is arguably one of the best albums of the 80s, if not all time, quite simply because, as strange and eerie it may be at some points, there's hardly a single moment you can't want to put yourself in Robert's shoes and think of what on Earth made him write these songs. It can make you believe that the song that you're listening to comes from exactly what it sounds like but if you listen close enough, you won't ever be able to understand what it really means, or maybe, you won't really want to know what led to them. But this album is a masterpiece in the way it sounds, the feel it carries, the places it takes you to and that every time you listen to it, it coughs up an entirely new interpretation in your mind.

The album begins with Plainsong, a song that I would imagine plays when you're being carried into heaven after you've died. And right after it throws you into Pictures Of You, which is a beautifully sad song, possibly about regrets with a past love interest or maybe even an obsessive necrophilic person. And that's precisely why The Cure is so brilliant; you just never know with them! There's also tracks like Lovesong which has got to be one of my favourite love songs, even though there is a lingering The Cure-esque creeps in this track too. I believe this song was written by Robert for his wife at the time though, so I'm sure the creeps were all with good intent. There isn't a single song on this album that I don't like and there are very few albums for which I feel that way. Right from start to finish.

Seriously though, how can songs as creepy as Lullaby, as gripping as Fascination Street, as romantic as Lovesong, as majestic as Plainsong and as wonderfully ordinary as Untitled be put together in one album? It's The Cure at their peak, don't think so much, just go wherever the music takes you and be grateful it's there at all.
