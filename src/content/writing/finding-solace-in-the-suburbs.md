Pictured are the band Deerhunter. I feel like the picture does a good job of representing the band - there's a lot of dreamy night-like qualities to their music but there's also shades of grey, lyrics dealing with loneliness and isolation. The music is transcendental at times... What differentiates them from other dream pop bands is their willingness to experiment, while maintaining a strong melodic sense. Singer and composer Bradford Cox has an ear for catchy melodies. Couple that with intricate and painstakingly handled production and you have albums that are often as stunning as they can get eerie.

Excerpt from an article - 'As a teenager, he (Bradford) dropped out of high school and his parents divorced, leaving him "to live in my childhood home alone. I literally lived in this large suburban house by myself."

With no friends, Cox became interested in how music could sound "heartbreaking or nostalgic or melancholy".'

The lyrics are often about finding comfort in isolation, with the self. The production is cozy, comforting and psychedelic. The nostalgia is reflected too... In 'Walkabout' from his solo project he sings 'What did you want to see, what did you want to be when you grew up?' in a repetitive fashion, driving home the point as the music gets more and more ethereal. The repetitive element is handled so well in other songs too (the refrain 'to get older still' in the song Little Kids, for example). The lyrics often make one crave for the times past, for the time when responsibilities were few and there was so much to explore, to discover; also a time of confusion and being overwhelmed at times. To conclude, if you could relate to themes I talked about here, you definitely will enjoy their music! Link to the music in a playlist in the bio.
